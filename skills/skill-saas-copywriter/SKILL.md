<objective>
Equip the AI assistant with copywriter strategies to write compelling, conversion-focused, and premium copy for SaaS product layouts (landing pages, pricing tiers, empty states, and notification messages).
</objective>

<process>

1. **Craft High-Converting Hero Sections**:
   - **Headline Formula**: State the ultimate value or action using strong verbs.
     - *Good*: "Repurpose Content 10x Faster" or "Turn One Piece of Content Into Dozens."
     - *Avoid*: "The AI content tool you need."
   - **Subheadline Formula**: Clarify how it works and mention the target benefit/user.
     - *Example*: "Turn blogs, videos, newsletters, and social posts into dozens of optimized LinkedIn, Twitter, and script assets in seconds."
   - **CTA buttons**: Use actionable, low-friction text:
     - *Primary*: "Start Free Trial" or "Generate for Free".
     - *Secondary*: "Watch Demo" or "See Examples".

2. **Structure Feature Copy (Benefits over Features)**:
   - For every feature card (e.g. Bento grid cards), write a short bold header followed by a one-sentence value statement:
     - *AI Content Generator*: "Generate multiple social formats instantly from a single URL."
     - *Workspace Management*: "Manage multiple brands and profiles under a single roof."
     - *Content Library*: "Organize, schedule, and filter your assets in one central repository."

3. **Refine Pricing Card Copy**:
   - **Starter**: "Perfect for creators starting to build their online presence."
   - **Pro**: "For growing businesses and agencies wanting to scale content output."
   - **Agency**: "For teams managing multiple brands and client accounts."
   - Emphasize benefits under each tier (e.g., "5 workspaces included", "Unlimited AI suggestions", "Priority rendering").

</process>

<notes>
- Avoid overly generic corporate jargon ("synergy", "cutting-edge solution"). Use simple, active verbs.
- Maintain a consistent, modern brand voice: confident, helpful, and highly efficient.
</notes>
